

# Beyond Jeffery: finite-inertia dynamics of slender fibers in general linear flows 

An MPI-parallel Fortran framework for computing the translation, rotation, hydrodynamic force distribution, and disturbance flow generated by a slender fiber moving in a general linear velocity field using an inertial linear-flow slender-body theory (SBT).

## Overview

This repository contains the numerical implementation of an inertial slender-body theory for a slender fiber undergoing simultaneous translation and rotation in a general linear imposed flow.

The formulation extends inertial SBT for steady fiber-fluid translation (Joshi et. al., arXiv: 2607.02993[physics.flu-dyn]) by retaining the transient evolution of the disturbance flow in a reference frame attached to the fiber. It combines:

* a transient, linearized Navier–Stokes outer solution at the scale of the fiber length,
* a steady, quasi-two-dimensional full Navier–Stokes inner solution at the scale of the fiber diameter,
* asymptotic matching between the inner and outer regions,
* a numerical solution of the outer disturbance in Fourier space along wave-vector characteristics,
* an integral equation for the force per unit length along the fiber axis, and
* the translational and rotational equations governing the fiber motion.

The method captures fluid-inertia effects associated with fiber translation, fiber rotation, and convection by an imposed linear flow without directly resolving the fiber cross-section on a three-dimensional physical-space grid. 

## Physical and Numerical Framework

At the fiber-length scale, the transient linearized Navier–Stokes equations are solved in Fourier space. The wave-vector and disturbance-velocity equations are integrated along characteristic trajectories. The resulting outer disturbance is matched to the inner solution (on the scale of fiber cross-section) through the inertial SBT integral equation.

The Fourier-space computational domain is represented by a spherical grid that is distributed across MPI processes using domain decomposition. The radial grid spacing may be clustered near k = 0 using a user-specified clustering parameter, allowing enhanced resolution of the small-wavenumber region without uniformly refining the entire Fourier-space domain. Most inter-process communication occurs during evaluation of the inverse Fourier transform of the velocity disturbance induced by the fiber an the ambient flow. The Fourier-space velocity disturbance is integrated over the spherical grid using the one-third Simpson’s rule over the spherical grid, with the integration distributed across MPI ranks. The corresponding real-space velocity disturbance is evaluated specifically along the fiber axis and substituted into the SBT integral equation used to determine the force distribution and fiber motion. 

## Features 

* MPI-parallel Fortran implementation
* finite-inertia slender-body theory for general linear flows
* simultaneous fiber translation and rotation
* transient disturbance-flow evolution in a fiber-attached frame
* Fourier-space solution using the method of characteristics
* inner–outer asymptotic matching
* distributed evaluation of the inverse Fourier transform
* prescribed or freely evolving fiber motion
* support for finite fiber inertia or force- and torque-free motion
* calculation of the force per unit length along the fiber
* output of fiber position, orientation, translational velocity, angular velocity, and hydrodynamic force

## Applications 

The code has been used to study:

1. **Freely settling fiber in a quiescent fluid**  
The coupled orientation, rotation rate, and settling velocity of a fiber are computed and compared with quasi-steady models based on Stokesian and Oseen-flow rotational resistance.
2. **Neutrally buoyant fiber in an extensional flow**  
The rotation of a fiber toward the stable extensional direction is computed, allowing finite-inertia corrections to the Stokes-limit angular-velocity law to be quantified.
3. **Neutrally buoyant fiber in simple shear flow**  
The code resolves the slow inertial drift of a fiber across Jeffery orbits. The drift rate and long-time orientational behavior can be studied as functions of the shear Reynolds number and fiber aspect ratio.
4. **Sedimenting fiber in simple shear flow**  
The combined effects of gravitational settling, background shear, fiber inertia, and fluid inertia produce orientation dynamics that differ substantially from classical Jeffery orbits. The code can be used to identify distinct dynamical regimes and quantify how they depend on the relative strengths of gravity and shear.

More broadly, the framework can be applied to:

* transient recovery toward equilibrium settling orientations,
* inertial modifications to Jeffery-type dynamics,
* fibers translating and rotating in homogeneous linear flows,
* coupled effects of gravity, strain, vorticity, and finite fluid inertia,
* phase maps and stability boundaries for fiber orientation dynamics.

## Scientific Scope 

This repository is intended for research on:

* hydrodynamics of slender particles at finite Reynolds numbers,
* transient particle–fluid interactions,
* sedimentation and orientation dynamics of fibers,
* departures from classical Stokes-flow and Jeffery-orbit predictions,
* inertial effects in homogeneous linear flows,
* reduced-order modeling of elongated particles without fully resolving their thickness.

The formulation assumes a slender particle and represents the fiber by a line distribution of force. Users should consult the accompanying paper for the asymptotic assumptions, applicable Reynolds-number ranges, numerical convergence requirements, and details of the inner solution.

## Source Code Release Status

The source code required to compile and run the solver is not included in the current public version of this repository. The build and run instructions below apply to the planned source-code release following publication of the associated paper.

## Build Requirements

Typical requirements will include:

* a Fortran compiler with MPI support,
* an MPI implementation,
* BLAS and LAPACK,
* Make,
* MATLAB or Python (optional but recommended, for post-processing) 

The code has been developed for distributed-memory execution on Linux-based computing systems.

## Build 

Compile the code from the repository root using

```bash
make
```

This generates the executable

```text
FiberLinearFlow_prog
```

To remove compiled files, use

```bash
make clean
```

\## Run 

A simulation may be launched with

```bash
mpirun -np <number_of_processes> ./FiberLinearFlow_prog
```

For example,

```bash
mpirun -np 32 ./FiberLinearFlow_prog
```

An example run script is provided in `scripts/`.

## Example Cases 

1\. **Freely settling fiber in a quiescent fluid**: This case follows the transient translation and rotation of a freely settling fiber in an otherwise quiescent fluid.

2\. **Neutrally buoyant fiber in extensional flow**: This case considers a neutrally buoyant fiber rotating toward the stable extensional direction in a planar extensional flow.

3\. **Neutrally buoyant fiber in simple shear**: This case can be used to quantify inertial drift across Jeffery orbits as a function of shear Reynolds number and fiber aspect ratio.

4\. **Freely settling fiber in simple shear flow**: This case examines the competing effects of gravity and shear on fiber orientation, rotation, and long-time dynamical behavior.

## Input parameters 

The principal user-defined parameters include:

* fiber aspect ratio,
* fluid density and kinematic viscosity,
* fiber density and inertia,
* imposed velocity-gradient tensor,
* gravitational acceleration,
* initial fiber position and orientation,
* initial translational and angular velocities,
* Fourier-space grid limits and resolution,
* time-step size and final simulation time,
* number of MPI processes,
* convergence tolerances,
* output frequency.

## Output 

Depending on the selected case, the code outputs quantities such as:

* simulation time,
* fiber centroid position,
* fiber orientation vector,
* translational velocity,
* angular velocity,
* hydrodynamic force and torque,
* force distribution along the fiber axis,
* selected Fourier-space or physical-space disturbance-flow quantities.

## Example simulation cases

The `examples/` directory contains the results for fiber motion from some of the representative cases of fiber in a linear flow field. 

Because the Fourier-space calculation can be computationally intensive, production cases may require multiple MPI processes and substantial memory. Users should perform grid, time-step, and Fourier-domain convergence tests before interpreting new parameter regimes.
